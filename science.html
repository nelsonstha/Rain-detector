Key Scientific Concepts

1. Electrical Conductivity of Water

Pure water is a poor conductor. Rainwater and tap water contain dissolved ions (salts, minerals, acids) that allow electric current to flow.

2. Completing the Circuit

When water bridges the two metal blades, it creates a low-resistance path → current flows → transistor turns ON → buzzer sounds.

3. Transistor as an Electronic Switch

The BC547 (NPN transistor) acts as a switch:

  • No water → very little base current → transistor OFF
  • Water present → base current flows → transistor ON → collector-emitter path allows current to buzzer

4. Why We Need a Transistor?

The current through water is very small (microamperes). The transistor amplifies this tiny current to control a larger current needed by the buzzer.

5. Related Physical Quantities

  • Resistance of water bridge: typically 1 kΩ – 50 kΩ (depending on distance & water type)
  • Voltage drop across sensor
  • Current gain (hFE) of transistor (~100–800)

Water acts as variable resistor in the base circuit of the transistor
