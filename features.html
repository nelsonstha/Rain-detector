Main Features & Advantages

  • Very low cost – total cost under NPR 300–500
  • Simple circuit – only 1 transistor, few resistors, no microcontroller needed
  • Instant response – buzzer sounds immediately when water touches sensor
  • Easy to build – can be completed in 30–60 minutes on a breadboard
  • Educational value – clearly demonstrates conductivity, transistors, and amplification
  • Customizable sensor – can use different blade spacing or materials
  • Expandable – can add LED, relay, timer, solar power, Arduino, etc.
  • No complex programming – 100% analog electronics
  • Visible & audible alert – good for science fair demonstration
  • Real-world relevance – principle used in actual rain sensors & automatic wipers

Limitations (Honest Science Fair Point)

  • Sensitive to very dirty / salty water (may give false alarms)
  • Does not distinguish between rain, dew, or someone spraying water
  • Basic version has no adjustable sensitivity
  • Buzzer is loud – good for demo, but might need volume control for home use
